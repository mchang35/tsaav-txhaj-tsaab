Obituary

Txaav Txhaj Tsaab (Sia Chang) was born on May 15, 1955, in the village of Taj Nyom near Naaj Xaj, Xiengkhouang Province, Laos to You Mai Chang (Nai Koo Ntsum Maiv Tsaab) and Sai Yang (Ntxhais Yaaj). He was the pride of his family and village as their first to receive a formal education. He completed his college education at the top of his class, and, other than his native Hmong language, was fluent in English, Lao and French. The Secret War in Laos cut short his career path upon graduation. In 1975, he led his familyâ€™s escape from war as internal refugees and fled across the Mekong River to Thailand. After a brief stay in Ban Vinai refugee camp where he married his lifelong partner Maim Haam, his family resettled in Honolulu, Hawaii and then moved to Minnesota. In Minnesota, he attended night school to obtain his GED and eventually landed a teaching job with the Minneapolis Public Schools, where he proudly served thousands of English as a Second Language (ESL) students for over 25 years. A son, a brother, a husband, a father, and grandfather, Txaav Txhaj was a man of few words, but lived a life that is rooted in love for his family and bounded by deep convictions about compassion, loyalty and service to family and community.

Txaav Txhaj is survived by his wife, Mai Hang (Maim Haam); daughters and sons-in-law Alee and Nyia Vang (Nyiaj Vaj), Betty and Geenou Yang (Ntsum Maiv Yaaj), and Chua (Cua); sons and daughters-in-law Dao (Txawj Tuam) and Wendy Yang, Fue (Fwv) and Ma Xiong (Mab Xyooj), and Xung (Xaab); grandchildren Fwjchim, Khais, Kayhli, Kalina, Madison, Corey, Gwenyth, and Marilyn; siblings Mai Song (Maiv Ntxhoo) and brother-in-law Joua Chou Xiong (Ntsuab Txus Xyooj), Xiong Pao (Laj Pov) and sister-in-law Pang Thao (Paaj Thoj), Yee (Nyaj Yig) and sister-in-law Mee Moua (Mim Muas), Chan (Fwm Txaam) and sister-in-law Angelica Keo, Khoua (Qhua) and brother-in-law Chris Bergstrom, Doua (Neej Nruag) and sister-in-law Yeng Lor (Yeev Lauj); and his many nephews and nieces. Txaav Txhaj was preceded in death by his parents You Mai (Ntsum Maiv) and Sai Yang (Ntxhais Yaaj) and by his eldest sister Xa (Rhav) and brother-in-law Chong Chou Thao (Ntxoov Tsu Thoj).
